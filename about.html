About ReMitts

How They're Made

How does ReMitts turn old sweaters, destined for the landfill, into new mittens?

  • Wool sweaters are donated by Madison-area thrift stores and ReMitts’ supporters.
  • Volunteers launder the sweaters in hot water and dry them in hot dryers, causing the sweaters to shrink or “felt.”
  • Pattern pieces are cut from the felted wool, combining complementary colors and designs.
  • The pieces are machine sewn into mittens into women’s, men’s and children’s sizes.
  • Each pair is lined with cozy fleece.
  • Women’s and children’s mittens are embellished with buttons, which are also donated.

How They Began

ReMitts started when Janet Tupy admired “upcycled” mittens – mittens made from old sweaters – at a gift shop. “I remembered reading about a pastor in a small church who gave each member of the congregation $100 with the charge to ‘grow’ the money and donate the proceeds,” said Janet. “I thought, ‘What could I do with $100 dollars of seed money? How much could I grow for a good cause?’” When she saw the upcycled mittens, she knew immediately what she could do with $100. Even though she hadn’t sewn in many years, she thought, “I could do this!” She gathered a few friends, they found a pattern, bought some wool sweaters at a thrift store, and made their first mittens. The year was 2009, and their goal was to raise $1,000 to donate to local food pantries. They raised $4,000. Fast forward to 2016, when the small group of friends, which grew to approximately two dozen friends, raised more than $50,000. Altogether, ReMitts has raised more than $225,000 for local food pantries. By taking something that was destined for the landfill and repurposing it, ReMitts and the customers who buy the mittens are feeding hungry people.

Care: Keep your ReMitts mittens clean by handwashing in cold water with mild soap. Air dry. Fluff in dryer on very low heat for five to 10 minutes.
